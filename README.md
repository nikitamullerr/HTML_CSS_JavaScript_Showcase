# TutorLink

A student tutoring platform that connects tertiary students with verified, affordable tutors — matched to their course, learning style, and budget.

---

## About the Project

TutorLink was built as part of the HTML, CSS + JavaScript Showcase Brief at Life Choices Academy (YouthCode programme). Our team of five came up with the idea after thinking about a real problem students face: finding a tutor is harder than it should be. Most platforms are either too expensive, unverified, or just throw you a random list with no real filtering.

So we built something better.

---

## The Problem

Tertiary students who need academic help often can't find tutors that are both affordable and trustworthy. On top of that, most existing platforms don't factor in things like learning style, personality, or course-specific knowledge — so students end up with the wrong tutor and waste both time and money.

---

## Our Solution

TutorLink matches students to tutors based on:
- The course they need help with
- Their learning style (visual, auditory, reading/writing, kinesthetic)
- Their personality preference
- Their availability and schedule
- Their budget

Every tutor on the platform is verified, and pricing is upfront — no surprises.

---

## Mission

To help students achieve academic success by connecting them with trusted and affordable tutors.

---

## Target Audience

Tertiary students who need subject-specific academic support and want a match that actually makes sense for how they learn.

## Features

**Home Page**
The landing page introduces TutorLink and gives users a clear overview of what the platform offers, with calls to action that guide them through the site.

**Find a Tutor**
The core feature of the platform. Students can filter through a catalog of verified tutors by course, price range, availability, and learning style to find the right match for them.

**About Us**
Covers who we are, what TutorLink stands for, and the mission behind the platform.

**Testimonials**
Success stories from students who've used TutorLink. The best way to build trust is to show real results.

**Contact Us**
A contact form with validation so students and tutors can reach out to the TutorLink team directly.

## Challenges

Git was probably the biggest learning curve for us as a team. We ran into merge conflicts a few times, mostly because multiple people were touching shared files at the same time without coordinating first. It caused some headaches but also taught us a lot about how important communication is when you're all working on the same codebase. We got better at it as the project went on.

---

## What We Learned

- Talk to your team before you code, not after — it saves a lot of conflict resolution
- Branching properly makes collaboration way less painful than working directly on main
- Following the SDLC process, especially the planning and Figma stages, makes the actual development smoother
- Version control is a real skill, not just something you set up once and forget

---
